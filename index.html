How Lido Staking Works

1) Connect Wallet & Choose Asset

Use MetaMask, Rabby, or a hardware wallet via WalletConnect. Select ETH to stake and confirm you have ETH for gas.

2) Receive stETH or wstETH

Stake ETH to mint stETH (rebasing token) or wrap it into wstETH (non-rebasing with accrued value). Choose stETH for simplicity, wstETH for DeFi integrations that prefer fixed balances.

3) Security & Protections

Verify the official Lido app URL, review tx prompts, and beware of fake airdrops. Keep allowances minimal, and prefer hardware wallets for large balances.

4) Withdraw or Use in DeFi

Request withdrawals back to ETH via the Lido queue (subject to network limits) or deploy stETH/wstETH in lending, LPing, or restaking strategies.

Safety & Best Practices

Liquid staking is powerful but carries protocol and market risks. Use these practices to stay safe.

Practical Protections

  • Use official links: Bookmark the Lido app; beware of phishing and fake support.
  • stETH vs wstETH: Prefer wstETH in DeFi that doesn’t support rebasing; unwrap to stETH if needed.
  • Allowance hygiene: Grant minimal approvals; periodically revoke on Etherscan.
  • Diversify venues: For large DeFi positions, spread across protocols and monitor health factors.
  • Hardware wallets: Keep keys offline; verify on-device details before signing.
  • Withdrawal planning: Withdrawal queue can fluctuate; maintain an ETH buffer for urgent needs.

Compliance & Risks

Staking involves market risk (ETH price, LSD discounts/premiums) and protocol risk (smart contracts, validator performance, slashing). Keep records for accounting/tax and ensure your strategy fits your risk tolerance and jurisdiction.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What’s the difference between stETH and wstETH?

stETH rebases (balance changes daily), while wstETH is non-rebasing with a value that appreciates vs. stETH. Many DeFi protocols prefer wstETH.

How do rewards accrue?

Rewards from the validator set accrue to stETH holders via rebases; wstETH reflects the same yield through an increasing exchange rate vs. stETH.

Can stETH depeg from ETH?

stETH is not hard-pegged. Market price can deviate based on liquidity, demand, and withdrawal queue dynamics. Monitor liquidity when entering or exiting positions.

How do withdrawals work?

Submit a withdrawal request to redeem stETH for ETH. Fulfillment depends on validator exits and queue length. Urgent exits may use secondary markets instead.

What are the fees?

A protocol fee is applied to staking rewards (not principal) and is shared with node operators and the treasury. Gas costs apply to staking/unstaking actions.

Is there slashing risk?

Yes. Validator misbehavior can incur slashing. Lido’s diversified node operator set and policies aim to reduce concentration and operational risk, but it cannot be eliminated.

Which wallets work best?

MetaMask or Rabby for daily use; pair with a Ledger/Trezor for large stakes. Always keep some ETH for gas.

Can I use stETH/wstETH in DeFi?

Yes. They’re widely integrated across lending markets, DEXs, and structured products. Check protocol-specific collateral factors and risk parameters.

My transaction failed — what now?

Common causes: insufficient gas, RPC hiccups, or allowance issues. Increase gas, refresh RPC, re-approve if needed, and retry with a small test amount.
